Struggle

By lomi

Nations that struggle the most with poverty are often the loudest ones about fighting corruption. Yet, decades pass, the same promises are repeated, the same scandals show, and the same people suffer. It makes you wonder if a poor country can ever truly escape corruption, or if the country is permanently tied to it together.

Poverty and corruption don't exist separately. They feed each other. The poor are trapped the system that reward dishonesty, while those in power benefit from keeping things exactly as they are. Until accountability overwrites excuses, no amount of help or economic growth can erase the stain of corruption.

Escaping corruption isn't impossible, but it's not easy either. It takes more than economic growth, it takes courage and strength. True reform means protecting independent courts, supporting a free press, and letting people see where their bills is going.

Education also plays a deeper role here. It's not just about teaching math or science it's about teaching integrity, responsibility, and empathy. The fight against corruption starts not in the government office, but in the classroom and at home. A nation cannot clean its own system if the people continue to tolerate the dirt.
