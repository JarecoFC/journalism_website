Editorial

By Noctilucent

The Philippines is a country that fought for its own sense of nationalism and freedom, freedom that countless of people have sacrificed their lives all for the sake of a better tomorrow for the people of the future generations. They knew they were out-matched, out-gunned and out-numbered but they kept fighting tooth and nail to ensure that a better and peaceful life is within the grasp of the Filipinos of the future generations to come. And now we live in an era of mostly peace, where most world leaders would opt to resolve situations through diplomatic means yet that goal for a better livelihood for Filipinos is still far out of reach and it’s not because of outsiders ironically It’s because of our fellow Filipinos that are up on the top.

Disdain from the common Filipino folk towards the leading government isn’t an entirely new thing, though there has been a recent realization from a few yet many Filipinos that have had enough of the betrayal of the government that was entrusted to serve the country. The government which is supposedly a country’s symbol of hope is now just a self-sustaining hungry machine that embraces its greed. This awakening led to the recent rally / protest that occurred that showed and vocalized the frustrations, anger and disappointment of the Filipinos towards the people that the common folk pay a cut of their income towards. That many people paying their taxes, taken directly away from the money that was supposed to feed their families and yet they barely see or touch the fruits that were promised to them. Decade old problems worsening, which makes the day to day struggle of a Filipino even harder than it has to be. Picture a man who is trying to earn a living walking the same sidewalk for years, as the amount of taxes he has paid almost form a mountain so does the sidewalk turn into crooked rubble more akin to a crater. The anger, the frustration, the cries during the day of the rally is barely scratches the surface of what has been building up inside these people.

What’s more surprising is how they mock us, belittle us, see us as mindless dolls to fill their pockets while they sit around and do nothing; actually they do less than nothing as they even prevent from projects aimed for the welfare of Filipinos from happening to make sure they squeeze as much money as they can from us. They flaunt their wealth as if it was truly theirs, showing luxury clothing and cars as they post going to famous events. While a school in the main capital is barely accommodating the students they have, a local hospital’s building showing cracks on its walls and even a whole city that accepts daily floods in their neighborhoods as the norm.

What they do not know, is that we are not powerless.

We cannot allow our country to be siphoned of its riches, the common folk bled dry for the ever consuming machine of greed and self-interest that we now call our government. If we are not to act then we cannot expect things to get better as in reality, it will only get worse from here. We’ve waited more than long enough for the government to right its wrongs, to prove to use that they are competent leaders capable of serving for the welfare of all as that has been proven wrong for so many times. We must not stand idly and we should take this amalgamation of greed off of its seat and reclaim the government that is meant to be for the people and serves the people.

VOX VERITAS
