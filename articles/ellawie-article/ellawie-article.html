Bare Feet

Many cross the flood with nothing but courage. Their steps tell stories of survival in silence.

Some people are trying to make their way through the waist-deep flood, holding umbrellas as they move along. Others are barefoot, clutching their belongings tightly, just trying to get through the water. You can feel how hard it is for them, but they keep walking because they don't really have a choice. It's sad, because this shouldn't always happen if only the money for protection and safety was used properly. Instead, corruption ate it up and now ordinary families are left to suffer. As you can see people are trying hard to push their motorcycles through the flood. The rain is pouring, and you can really feel the struggle on their faces. They should've been riding safely on the road, but now they're stuck fighting against the water. This makes me think of how corruption leaves people unprotected. The projects that could have prevented this are gone, stolen by greedy hands. Just like them, justice is also stuck, moving slowly through the flood.
